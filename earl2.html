About Her
Lea Salonga was born in Pampanga, with her father being Feliciano Genuino (an engineer/owner of shipping company), and Maria Ligaya (a homemaker and manager). She is one of two children, with her younger brother, Gerard Salonga (a musical director/conductor). At a young age, she was shown to be musically talented, starring in Annie (1980) as the titular protagonist at only 7 years old, and other films such as "The Sound of Music (1980)", "Fiddler on the Roof (1978)", "The Rose Tattoo (1980)," etc. Even as a child, she released her first album "Small Voice" that was certified gold in the Philippines."

As a teenager, she starred in many Broadway plays, two of the most notable ones being "Les Miserables" and "Miss Saigon", and provided singing voices for popular Disney Princesses such as Mulan and Jasmine. She recieved many awards for Her performance during her time in the spotlight.

At first, she planned to become a doctor, but after everyone recognized her talent, she stayed in the music industry, making many profitable roles and releasing many songs during her lifetime.

She now has a loving husband and an only child named "Nicole", and is an inspiration to many people around the world for her gift of music and acting.

Fun Facts about Lea Salonga:

Lea Salonga is a huge fan of the K-Pop band BTS:

Something that Lea Salonga has admitted is her admiration for the popular Korean boy band BTS. According to her online activity, she has stated that she has admired Jungkook, one of the members of the band for his musical talent and dedication to his artistic venture. She also stated that Kim Tae-Hyung is the most handsome person in the planet (in her opinion). We can say that nowadays, BTS is such a megastar in the media that not even celebrities are safe from falling head over heals for the baby-faced dance and their performance.

Her favorite lead man is Aga Muhlach:

When asked, Lea Salonga herself has stated that her most favorite lead man is the actor Aga Muhlach. It is most likely due for knowing Aga early in her acting career, as she co-starred with him in Filipino classics such as "Bakit Labis Kitang Mahal (1992)", and "Sana Maulit Muli (1995)".
